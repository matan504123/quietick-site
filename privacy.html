Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-05-26

Quietick is a haptic timer for Apple Watch and iPhone. This policy explains honestly and plainly what data the app handles — which is, in short, almost none.

What Quietick does not collect

Quietick collects no personal data, no analytics, no usage tracking, and no telemetry of any kind. The developer never sees who uses the app, how often it is used, which features are tapped, or anything else about your activity.

  • No analytics SDKs (no Firebase, no Amplitude, no Mixpanel).
  • No crash-reporting services (no Sentry, no Crashlytics).
  • No advertising networks or attribution tools.
  • No third-party SDKs of any kind are included in the app.

Your data stays yours

Content you create in Quietick — timer presets, session history, and custom haptic patterns — is stored locally on your device. If you have iCloud enabled, this data is synchronized between your own devices through Apple's iCloud (CloudKit). This synchronization happens entirely within your iCloud account and is not visible to or accessible by the developer.

Apple's own privacy policy governs how iCloud and CloudKit handle your data. You can review it at apple.com/legal/privacy.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or about how Quietick handles data, please email matan504@gmail.com. Questions are answered promptly.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the effective date at the top of this page will be updated and the new version will be posted at this same URL. No retroactive changes will be made to how previously collected data is handled — and since no data is collected, that means no changes to worry about.
